Spending

The Spending view shows where your money goes, and it keeps the two pots apart. They are different accounts in different currencies, so Daimond shows them as two tracks and never adds them together.

Two pots, kept apart

  • Inference. The model calls themselves, billed to your own provider key. Daimond prices and records them on this device, per turn, so you can see the cost of your own key spend without any of it passing through us.
  • Credits. The prepaid balance the gateway spends on the few things that leave the browser: fetching a page, syncing or sending mail, and cross-device sync.

Opening it

Open the Spending view from the spend meter in the top bar, or from a link in the Credits view. It shows each pot with a headline figure, a daily graph, a breakdown by category, and a plain table of movements, so a charge is never a mystery.

Protection from runaway costs

A workspace that can run many agents at once can, in principle, spend fast. The danger is not a large monthly total — it is a rate spike: a fan-out that burns through credit in seconds before you can react. Daimond is built to catch that spike, not merely to tally the total after the fact. Four measures work together, and none of them asks you to set a limit in advance.

  • A pace check before a fan-out runs. The cost of dispatching a batch of agents is known before any of them starts. When a batch would run faster than your own recent pace, Daimond pauses it and asks you once, with the number and the estimate on the prompt. A few agents of ordinary cost never reach it; a sudden fifty do. The measure of “too fast” is your own normal, learned from your recent spend, so there is nothing to configure.
  • Pause and stop, always to hand. The Agents panel lets you hold every running agent at a stroke, or stop them outright. Pausing stops the spend at once and keeps the work, so you lose nothing by pulling the brake to look.
  • A separate key for every agent. Each agent running on credits spends its own freshly minted key, and each key is capped against what is genuinely left after its siblings' claims. Because no two agents ever share a key, parallel agents cannot race a shared allowance past its cap — a whole class of overspend simply cannot happen.
  • Everything metered, per turn, on your device. Every model call is priced and recorded here as it happens, feeding the graphs above. The bill is never a surprise arriving later; it is in front of you as it is spent.

The intent is plain: run a team of agents without fear that a mistake — a runaway loop, a fan-out larger than you realised — can cost more than a glance and a click can undo.
