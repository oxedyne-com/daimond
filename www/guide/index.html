Getting started

Daimond is an AI agent workspace that runs entirely in your browser. There is no account on a server and no app to install: your chats, keys and files live only on this device.

Nothing leaves your device unless you direct it to. Daimond reaches the outside world only at your request: when you connect your own model provider, so your messages go to that provider; when you spend credits to run a model, fetch a page, or sync mail; and when you link a second device. Everything else stays in this browser, and Accounts & privacy sets out exactly what goes where, and how you can check it for yourself.

The shortest path to a first answer

Three steps. Each one points at a control you can see on screen.

  1. Create an account

    On first run Daimond offers to protect this device with a passphrase. Type your name and a passphrase, then choose Create passphrase. The passphrase encrypts your stored keys and mail credentials, and it is your on-device identity. It is never sent anywhere, so keep it somewhere safe — if you lose it, the encrypted data is gone.

    You may also choose Skip for now and set a passphrase later, but connecting a provider key works best once a passphrase is in place to protect it.

  2. Connect a model, or buy credits

    A daimon needs a model to think with. You have two ways to give it one, both reached from the admin panel at the bottom-left:

    • Bring your own key. Open the Models row, choose + Add provider, pick a provider, paste its API key, and star a default model. Your messages then go straight to that provider on your own account.
    • Buy credits. Open the Credits row and buy a pack. Daimond runs the model for you, with no provider key to manage and no subscription.

    Either one is enough. See Models & credits for the detail.

  3. Start a chat

    Choose New chat at the top of the rail (the ＋ beside Chats), type a message in the box at the bottom of the centre panel, and send it with ➤. That centre panel is the daimon — the conversation Daimond is built around.

What you are looking at

The screen has three zones. The rail on the left lists what you are working on and, below a movable divider, the admin panel. The stage in the centre holds the conversation and anything you are reading beside it. The dock on the right holds the sources you pull from — Email, the Workspace and live agent runs.

The admin panel is where Daimond is set up and where it tells you what it is costing. Each row is a button that opens the thing it names: your identity, Models, Credits, Tools, and the storage your workspace is using. The next page walks through every part of the screen.
