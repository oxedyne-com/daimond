Cross-device sync

Daimond is local-first, so a second device starts empty. Cross-device sync carries your chats and workspace files between your own devices without the server ever reading them.

What travels through the gateway is one encrypted parcel that only your devices hold the key to. The key comes from your passphrase and never leaves the browser, so the server stores the parcel but cannot open it. Nothing readable crosses between devices.

Linking a second device

On the device you already use, choose Link another device. It shows a one-time code and a QR code. You then have two ways to bring the new device in:

  • Scan the QR. On the new phone, point its camera at the QR code. It opens Daimond and links the device in one step, using the phone's own camera, with no in-app scanner to install.
  • Type the code. Open Daimond on the new device, choose Have a pairing code?, and type the one-time code shown on the first device.

Either way, you then unlock the new device with your usual passphrase, and your chats and files appear. A passkey can unlock it faster afterwards, once you add one there.

What it costs

Sync is free for now. When it is switched on it comes bundled into the one-time Pro unlock, not a subscription, so it stays bought once like the rest of Daimond.
